❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best exam to start with for a beginner? ▼
We recommend starting with SSC CHSL or SSC MTS as these are relatively easier than SSC CGL and provide a good foundation for competitive exams. These exams have less stringent eligibility criteria and help you understand the exam pattern better.
How many hours per day should I study for government exams? ▼
Ideally, 4-6 hours of dedicated study per day is recommended for effective preparation. However, consistency matters more than duration. It's better to study 3-4 hours daily with full concentration than 8 hours with distraction. Quality over quantity is key.
Can I prepare for multiple exams simultaneously? ▼
Yes, you can prepare for related exams together. For example, SSC and Banking exams share common topics in Quantitative Aptitude and English. We recommend focusing on one exam primarily while practicing common sections for other exams.
What is the ideal preparation duration? ▼
The preparation period depends on the exam difficulty and your background. For SSC exams: 3-6 months, Banking exams: 2-3 months, UPSC: 1-2 years, NDA/CDS: 4-6 months. Starting preparation 6-12 months before the exam gives you enough buffer time.
How do I track my progress effectively? ▼
Our analytics dashboard provides detailed insights into your test performance, weak areas, and improvement trends. Review your performance analytics weekly, identify weak topics, and allocate more time to those areas. This systematic approach helps you improve faster.
What happens if I fail in the exam? ▼
Most government exams don't have any restriction on number of attempts. You can attempt as many times as you wish (subject to age limit). Use our detailed performance analytics to identify weak areas and focus on improvement for the next attempt.
